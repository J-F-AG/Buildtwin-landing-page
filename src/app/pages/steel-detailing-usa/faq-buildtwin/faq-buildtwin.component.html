What are the stages of Steel Detailing in a construction project?

Steel detailing is a phase in steel structure construction that involves:

  • Planning and Design Review: Steel detailers work with architects and engineers to understand the project requirements and review the structural design.
  • Shop Drawings: Detailed shop drawings are created to guide steel fabrication. These drawings include dimensions, material specs and assembly instructions.
  • 3D Modeling and Visualization: Using Tekla Structures and Autodesk Revit detailers create 3D models of the structure to visualize the end result and detect potential issues early.
  • Fabrication Drawings: Fabrication drawings are created to guide steel fabricators with cutting, welding and assembly details.
  • Erection Drawings: These drawings are required for on-site assembly of steel components showing the placement, alignment and bolting of parts.
  • Quality Checks and Revisions: Multiple quality checks are done throughout the detailing process to ensure accuracy and industry standards compliance. Revisions may be made based on feedback from the construction team or design changes.
How do steel detailers ensure integration with other building systems?

Steel detailers play a crucial role in ensuring the steel structure integrates with other building systems like electrical, plumbing and HVAC. This is done through:

  • Clash Detection: Using BIM tools detailers can detect and resolve clashes with other building systems before construction starts. This proactive approach prevents conflicts and changes during the build phase.
  • Collaborative Workflows: Detailers often work with other trades during the 3D modeling phase to coordinate the placement and routing of various systems so all components fit together without interference.
  • Coordination Drawings: In addition to shop drawings, detailers create coordination drawings that include all building systems.
