Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Eurocode compliance and its services

What are Eurocodes?

Eurocodes are a comprehensive set of European standards governing structural design of buildings and other civil structures and, promoting safety, efficiency, and sustainability.

What is the role of EN 1990 in the design of structures Eurocodes?

EN 1990 outlines the principles, supplementary rules and general rules of structural design, providing the framework and general rules for safety, reliability, and durability in construction of steel and concrete structures.

What challenges do Eurocodes address in the construction sector?

Eurocodes mitigate risks from structural elements of construction products like material failure, structural instability, and environmental impacts during construction works, ensuring robust project outcomes.

Are Eurocodes suitable for small projects?

Absolutely. Even small projects benefit from the structured approach and resource efficiency promoted by Eurocodes.

How can Eurocodes improve cost efficiency?

Eurocodes optimize material usage and streamline processes, reducing waste, rework, and overall project costs.

Are Eurocodes mandatory?

While not mandatory in all regions, Eurocodes are widely recognized as best practices and are often required for international projects.

How do Eurocodes enhance sustainability?

By encouraging material efficiency, recycling, and eco-friendly design principles, Eurocodes align with green building goals and reduce environmental impacts.
