Why are building information models important?

BIM is important in the architecture, engineering and construction industries for:

  • Better Visualization: BIM allows you to create detailed 3D models so you can see the project before it’s built. This helps with decision making and planning.
  • More Collaboration: With one shared model BIM brings all stakeholders (architects, engineers, contractors) together, making project management easier.
  • Efficiency and Accuracy: BIM helps you catch conflicts and issues early in the design stage, reduces errors, saves time and minimizes costly changes during construction.
  • Cost Management: Cost and scheduling integrated into BIM (4D and 5D BIM) means you can budget and resource better and avoid overruns.
  • Lifecycle Management: Post construction BIM models become a resource for facility management, containing information useful for maintenance and operations. Overall BIM makes construction projects more efficient, accurate and manageable from conception to completion and beyond.

What is the difference between BIM and 3D CAD?

Both BIM and 3D CAD creates digital models of buildings but there is a big difference:

  • Data Integration: BIM models are data rich, each element has properties whereas 3D CAD models are geometry only.
  • Collaboration: BIM is real time collaboration with multiple stakeholders whereas 3D CAD is individual discipline.
  • Lifecycle Management: BIM is for entire lifecycle of a building from design to construction to operation and maintenance. 3D CAD is for design only.
  • Clash Detection and Analysis: BIM has advanced tools for clash detection and project analysis which is not present in traditional 3D CAD.
  • 4D, 5D and Beyond: BIM extends to time (4D), cost (5D), sustainability (6D), facility management (7D) and more, a more comprehensive approach than 3D CAD. In short BIM is a holistic process that brings collaboration, efficiency and decision making throughout the project lifecycle.

What is the BIM Level of Development (LOD) for my AEC project?

LOD for your project depends on many factors like project phase, complexity and specific requirements. LOD ranges from 100 to 500:

  • LOD 100 (Conceptual Design): Basic massing models to represent overall project intent.
  • LOD 200 (Schematic Design): Generalized systems and assemblies with approximate quantities, size, shape and location.
  • LOD 300 (Detailed Design): Precise modeling with specific assemblies, accurate quantities and dimensions for coordination.
  • LOD 350 (Construction Documentation): Interfaces between building elements with detailed coordination.
  • LOD 400 (Fabrication and Assembly): Models with complete fabrication and assembly information.
  • LOD 500 (As-Built): Represents the project as built for operations and maintenance.

How BuildTwin Helps: Our vendors can deliver BIM models at any LOD for your project. We recommend:

  • Early Phases: Start with LOD 100-200 for conceptual and schematic design.
  • Design Development: Move to LOD 300-350 for detailed coordination and construction documentation.
  • Construction and Fabrication: Use LOD 400 for fabrication details.
  • Facility Management: Use LOD 500 for as-built models for long term maintenance. Our experts can help you to decide the right LOD for your project and goals.
